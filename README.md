# Global-Student-Migration-Placement-Analytics (Tableau Project)

Project Overview
This project analyzes a dataset of 5,000 international students to identify trends in global migration, academic performance, and career outcomes. Using Tableau, I developed an interactive dashboard and data story to visualize the journey from enrollment to professional placement in top-tier global firms.

🛠️ Tools & Technologies
Data Visualization: Tableau Desktop / Tableau Public

Data Processing: Excel (Data Cleaning & Formatting)

📊 Key Insights
Placement Success: Achieved a 50.2% placement rate across the cohort, with significant hiring from firms like Microsoft, Google, and Amazon.

Academic Correlation: Analysis confirms a strong link between high GPA/Test Scores and scholarship eligibility.

Migration Trends: Identified the USA, Canada, and Ireland as the primary destination hubs for international talent between 2022 and 2024.

📊 Final Insights: Global Talent Migration & Career Outcomes
1. Trend Analysis: Graduation & Placement Volatility
Peak Growth: Student volume saw a steady increase from 2020, peaking in 2022 with 496 graduates.

Recent Decline: There is a notable downward trend starting after 2024, with a projected or current count of only 177 students for 2026. This suggests a need to investigate whether this is due to incomplete data for the current year or a genuine drop in migration/enrollment.

2. Career Success & Placement Metrics
Success Rate: The dashboard shows a 50% Career Success Rate, which is reflected in the nearly equal split between "Placed" (1,225) and "Not Placed" (1,198) students.

Hiring Landscape: * The vast majority of students are categorized under "Finding Job," indicating a high number of active job seekers compared to those already settled in major firms.

Among corporate placements, Microsoft, Amazon, and Google emerge as the top three recruiters, followed closely by Apple and Tesla.

3. Geographic Distribution & Migration Flow
Top Destinations: Russia (76), Ireland (73), and the UAE (73) are the leading destination countries for students in this dataset.

Diverse Reach: High talent mobility is also seen toward Germany and the UK, while the USA and Canada show slightly lower concentrations within this specific cohort.

4. Financial Support & Academic Performance
Scholarship Impact: The Financial Support Rate stands at 52%, indicating that over half of the student population relies on some form of scholarship or aid.

Academic Baseline: The average GPA is 3.25, suggesting a high-performing talent pool moving across borders.

5. Language Proficiency & Prerequisites
Test Distribution: Students utilize a variety of proficiency tests, with IELTS and Duolingo appearing as prominent choices for those receiving scholarships.

The "None" Factor: A significant segment of students falls under the "None" category for language tests, which may correlate with students moving from or to countries where their native language is the primary medium of instruction.

6. Key Recommendations for Stakeholders
Bridge the Placement Gap: Since 50% of students remain unplaced, strengthening university-to-corporate pipelines with the top-hiring firms (Microsoft/Amazon) could improve outcomes.

Targeted Support: With a 52% financial support rate, increasing scholarship availability for the "Law" field (the current filtered view) could further incentivize talent migration from the UK (the current origin filter).
